HOUR OF CODE
TEAM 35

Connecting with the Community
To choose a suitable community for our event, we analyzed multiple options, ultimately
selecting Secondary School No. 39 in Sumqayit.
Access to such events is limited there.
The school principal, Bakhsheish Alamshakhov, warmly welcomed us and helped
organize the event, scheduled for November 6th, from 3:00 PM to 4:00 PM.

Event Preparation: Before "Hour of Code"
We worked to inspire student participation,
briefing them on the event's educational value and the certificates they
could earn.
With the principal’s help, we informed various classes,
eventually selecting 20 students from the 5th and 7th grades.
Parental approval was secured for participation
and media coverage to avoid any issues.



Hosting the "Hour of Code"
The school provided essential equipment,
including computers, a projector, and internet access. Due to space
limitations, we split participants into two groups.
The session began with a presentation about ADA University by team
member Nurjahan, tailored to the students’ level. While the
projector had limited clarity, individual computers ensured students
understood the material.
The team assisted students throughout, resolving
difficulties and maintaining engagement.
Despite logistical challenges, students' enthusiasm made the effort worthwhile.



Post-Event: Certificate Distribution
At the event's conclusion, participants shared reflections and received certificates.
We captured
memorable moments with group photos,
encouraged students to excel in their studies, and addressed their
additional questions.
Before leaving,
we thanked the principal and staff for their support.
